Michael J. Huddleston (aka “ICT” — the Inner Circle Trader) 2016 mentorship series, including definitions of major concepts and how to apply them in live markets — especially for forex trading and your signals group. This is for educational purposes only, not financial advice.

1. Interbank Price Delivery Algorithm (IPDA)

Definition: The idea that the market (especially forex) is structured by institutions to deliver price in a patterned way, not randomly.

Live Use: Watch for institutional delivery moves — liquidity sweeps, rapid expansions. Align your trades with institutional flow instead of against it.

2. Market Structure: BOS, CHOCH, MSS

Definition: BOS = Break of Structure, CHOCH = Change of Character, MSS = Market Structure Shift. These define trend continuation or reversal points.

Live Use: Identify structure on higher timeframes. Use BOS for continuation bias and CHOCH/MSS for reversals or exits.

3. Order Blocks (OB)

Definition: The last bullish or bearish candle before a strong move — area where institutional orders sit.

Live Use: Mark these zones. When price returns, look for entries with structure confirmation.

4. Fair Value Gap (FVG)

Definition: Price inefficiency zone created during a sharp move, later rebalanced by price action.

Live Use: Watch for retraces into FVGs for entry confirmation or continuation setups.

5. Liquidity Pools & Sweeps

Definition: Zones where stop orders cluster (above highs, below lows). Sweeps trigger liquidity before reversals.

Live Use: Anticipate liquidity grabs and reversals after wicks or false breaks in these areas.

6. Discount & Premium Zones

Definition: Price above 50% of range is premium (sell), below 50% is discount (buy). Used with Fibonacci retracement.

Live Use: Align order blocks or FVGs within premium/discount zones for confluence setups.

7. Risk Management & Growth

Definition: Manage trades with defined risk (≤1% per trade). Prioritize setups with strong R:R ratios and journaling.

Live Use: Track outcomes, scale out at key zones, and always maintain control of drawdown.

Putting It All Together

Analyze higher timeframes for bias → Mark structure, OBs, FVGs, liquidity → Wait for sweep + displacement → Enter at confirmation zone → Manage risk and scale out.
